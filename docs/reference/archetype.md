## Proposed Design Direction
Working concept: `The Pulse Archive`.

Parallel exploration now in progress:
- `The Pulse Archive` in `slowdragv1.html`: slower, more tactile, paper/ink, archive-led.
- `Black Field / Living Signal` in `slowdragv2.html`: darker, more spatial, more motion-led, with floating video planes, kinetic type, and a central film stage inspired by the immersive digital-experience discipline seen in Lusion.

Thesis:
- Slow Drag should not behave like a standard portfolio.
- The website should reward slowness as an interaction principle.
- If the audience slows down, images breathe, copy appears, and the work becomes deeper; if they rush, the site withholds detail or collapses back into fragments.

Design personality:
- cinematic but not gimmicky
- intimate but commercially legible
- textured but controlled
- anti-template, but still easy to understand
- built around rhythm, resistance, remembrance, care, memory, and creative freedom

Core interaction ideas:
- A slow-scroll hero where the logo and showreel fragments breathe in pulse, not spectacle.
- A `tempo-sensitive` reveal system: lingering on a frame reveals deeper copy, collaborators, method, and context.
- A living archive grid where work is organized by emotional residue and form, not just by project type.
- Work cards behave like contact sheets: hover/focus plays short clips, drag scrubs frames, pause reveals intent.
- About section shows the method as overlap: conversation, listening, contamination across disciplines, making, release.
- Microcopy should feel like direction notes, not marketing filler.

Suggested sitemap:
- `Pulse` / Home
- `Practice` / About and how we work
- `Archive` / Work
- `Signals` / short content, experiments, fragments
- `Begin` / Contact and inquiry

First hero direction to explore next:
- No burning paper, no old TV, no generic full-screen effect.
- Use the actual Slow Drag logo, OffBit type, and a carefully chosen set of short video fragments.
- The hero should ask the audience to slow down through interaction: as the user scrolls or drags gently, the tagline `Rhythm. Resistance. Remembrance.` appears in phases, paired with brief image fragments and one precise line of positioning.
- The hero should communicate the studio’s offer within the first few seconds: film, design, and visual worlds for brands, artists, institutions, and movements.
